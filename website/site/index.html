The Great Ocean Road

The Great Ocean Road is an Australian National Heritage listed
240-kilometre stretch of road along the south-eastern coast of
Australia between the Victorian cities of Torquay and Allansford.

Apollo Bay

Apollo Bay is a coastal town located in southwestern Victoria, Australia. Apollo Bay offers both swimming and surf beaches, as well as a large boat harbour and marina which is home to a major fishing fleet. Overlooking Apollo Bay offers are rolling green hills, which create quite a scenic backdrop when viewed from the beach.

London Bridge

London Bridge is an offshore natural arch in the Port Campbell National Park, Australia. It's a hugely popular attraction with tourists who are looking to soak up the beauty of Australia and get to grips with its wild and wonderful landscape. The collapse of the London Bridge highlights the everchanging structure of this coast.

Split Point Lighthouse

The Split Point Lighthouse is located in Aireys Inlet, which is a small town on the Great Ocean Road. This is a must-see attraction and stands as a stunning sentinel on the Shipwreck Coast. It is still a vital wokring lighthouse for vessels navigating the treacherous waters of Bass Strait and still operates every night.

Memorial Arch at
Eastern View

The Memorial Arch was built in honour of the 3,000 returned soldiers who worked on the road and its creation during Worl War I. It is a popular destination to stop at. This is because it is the start of the Great Ocean Road. Many tourists will take the chance to stop at the designed carpark to take a photo with the arch.

Melbourne City

Melbourne is Australia's mecca for all things trendy and tasty. The city offers up exquisite dining, exhilarating sport and other abundant oppurtunities to experience art. Explore its trendy neighbourhoods, bustling laneways and sophisticated foodie scene to get a taste of what Melbourne is all about.

The Twelve Apostles

The Twelves Apostles is one of the Great Oceab Road's number one tourist highlight. Glorious limestone stacks emrging from the choopy seas, scattering along the coast for a breathtaking sight. The apostles formed millions of years ago, but sadly over time, the number of stacks have dropped.

Port Campbell

The holiday seaside town of Port Campbell is located on the scenic Great Ocean road. Port Campbell is a famous tourist attractions because it is closely surrounded by some of the Great Ocean Road's popular and scenic coastal attractions.

Lorne

Lorne is one of the first town on the Great Ocean Road in Victoria, Australia. It is a popular venue for surfing. When the population balloons in the summer season, it becomes a weekend getaway for the city dwellers in Melbourne. Popular activities include pier fishing, swimming,and camping.
